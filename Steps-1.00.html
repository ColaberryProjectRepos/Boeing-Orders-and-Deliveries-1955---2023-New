PROBLEM STATEMENT:

Boeing, a prominent aerospace manufacturer, has experienced a consistent decline in the average revenue per aircraft over the years. This decline raises concerns about the company's financial performance and its ability to remain competitive in the global market. Identifying the underlying factors contributing to this decline and understanding its implications is crucial for Boeing's strategic decision-making and long-term sustainability. According to Forbes.com, Boeing held a monopoly in aircraft supply until 1974 when Airbus entered the market, and both companies now dominate 91% of the global commercial aircraft market. However, Boeing has faced declining average revenue per aircraft over the years, in contrast to Airbus's stable average revenue.
